Privacy & Data

We respect your privacy and take steps to keep patient and visitor information safe. This page explains what we collect, why, and how you can exercise your rights.

Quick overview

We collect the minimum information needed to provide care and run our clinic. We do not store sensitive medical records on this public website. Contact forms are forwarded securely to our clinic inbox or your configured backend (e.g. Formspree). For clinical records and test results we store and protect data within our clinical systems — not on this website.

What we collect

  • Contact form data: name, phone, email, short message — used to respond and arrange appointments.
  • Appointment requests: preferred date, service and non-sensitive notes to schedule visits.
  • Technical logs: server logs and analytics (see Cookie Policy) — anonymous and used to improve the site.

How we use data

  • To respond to messages and confirm appointments.
  • To provide and coordinate care when you visit the clinic.
  • To improve our website and services using anonymous analytics (only with consent).
  • To comply with legal obligations and to respond to safety or public health concerns.

Legal basis & retention

We process your contact and appointment data to fulfil our contract with you (care & appointment scheduling) and for our legitimate interests (clinic administration). We keep contact and appointment records for the time required by local regulations and for safe clinical practice. Analytics data is kept in aggregated/anonymous form and retained for limited periods.

Security

We restrict access to clinical and contact data to trained staff only. Our site uses secure connections (HTTPS). Where third-party services (e.g. Formspree, email providers) are used, we choose reputable providers and limit the data we share. No sensitive clinical history or test results should be sent through the public contact form — call the clinic instead.

Third parties

We may share data with trusted service providers who support clinic operations (email, appointment systems, labs) under contract. We do not sell personal data. If we must transfer data outside Kenya, we require appropriate legal protections or safeguards from the provider.

Your rights

Depending on local laws, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of incorrect information.
  • Request deletion or restriction of processing (with exceptions for clinical care or legal obligations).
  • Object to processing for direct marketing.
  • Withdraw consent for optional cookies (analytics/marketing) at any time.

How to exercise these rights: Email kimsuspecialisthosp@gmail.com or visit reception. Please provide a clear request and proof of identity where needed.

Children

Our services are for adults. If you are a parent or guardian booking for a child, please let us know so we can take appropriate consent. We do not knowingly collect data from children on the website without parental consent.

Privacy FAQ

You can request deletion of personal data held by the clinic, subject to legal or clinical record-keeping obligations. Contact us via email or visit reception to start the request.

Contact forms are sent over secure HTTPS. Messages are forwarded to our clinic inbox or the configured backend (Formspree). Avoid sending detailed medical or sensitive personal information via the contact form.

Email kimsuspecialisthosp@gmail.com or ask reception to escalate your query. We will respond to privacy requests within a reasonable period.

Need help with privacy?

If you wish to exercise a right, ask a question, or make a complaint about how we handle data, contact our team.
