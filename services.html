Our Services

We focus on urology today and are expanding into more specialties soon. Below you will find short, clear descriptions of the services we offer and what you can expect at your visit.

Featured: Urology

Our main specialty — we treat urinary tract infections, kidney stones, prostate problems, and other urinary concerns. We use gentle, evidence-based tests and procedures to find the cause and help you recover quickly.

Typical care steps

  1. Consult: Speak with a urology clinician about symptoms and history.
  2. Tests: On-site ultrasound, urine tests, or blood tests as needed.
  3. Treatment: Medication, simple procedures, or referral for surgery if required.
  4. Follow-up: A nurse or doctor checks progress and advice for home care.

Urology

Consultation, diagnostics and minimally invasive procedures.

We diagnose and treat problems like kidney stones, urinary infections, blood in urine, and prostate symptoms. Tests include urine tests, blood tests and ultrasound. Minor procedures (like rigid or flexible endoscopy) are done by specialists.

Real-life example: If you have sudden severe back pain and blood in urine, we may do an ultrasound and urine tests the same day, then plan treatment (medicine or stone removal).

Endoscopy & Diagnostics

Safe outpatient endoscopy and quick diagnostic reports.

Endoscopy lets us look inside the urinary tract using a thin camera. Most procedures are same-day and done under local or light sedation. We follow strict cleaning and safety steps.

Real-life example: A patient with recurring infections might have an endoscopy to check the bladder and urethra for a cause.

Outpatient Care

Follow-up visits, medication checks and referral coordination.

We run follow-up clinics to check recovery and adjust medication. If a patient needs surgery or specialist care elsewhere we help with coordinated referrals and notes.

Real-life example: After stone removal, patients return for a follow-up visit to check wounds and give advice for preventing future stones.

New specialties (coming soon)

Nephrology, general surgery and more — watch for announcements.

We plan to add kidney specialists and general surgical services. These will be added when we have trained staff and necessary equipment in place.

What to expect at your visit

  1. Check-in: Front desk confirms your details and insurance (if any).
  2. Consultation: The clinician asks about symptoms and medical history.
  3. Tests: On-site urine or blood tests and ultrasound if needed.
  4. Plan: We explain treatment options and next steps in plain language.

How to prepare

  • Bring your ID and any medicine list.
  • If you have test results from other clinics bring copies or photos.
  • For some procedures you may need to fast — our staff will tell you beforehand.

Quick FAQ

Do you accept insurance? Yes — please call reception to confirm your plan.

How long is a typical visit? A follow-up visit is often 15–30 minutes; initial consultations may take up to an hour if tests are needed.

Do you offer emergency care? For severe or life-threatening emergencies, call the ambulance or go to the nearest full hospital. We offer urgent outpatient consults where possible.
